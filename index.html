Charles Kondek - "A shrewd man of the theater" Chicago Tribune

CHARLES KONDEK is the recipient of the prestigious MARC BLITZSTEIN MEMORIAL AWARD IN MUSIC THEATER presented by the American Academy of Arts and Letters.

ESTHER, written with HUGO WEISGALL and premiered by the New York City Opera at Lincoln Center, was proclaimed by New York Magazine as “a work that can now be placed among the very finest operas we have,” and Time Magazine termed ESTHER “a powerful evening of music theatre.” Martin Bernheimer of the Los Angeles Times suggested ESTHER “may be a masterpiece.”

KONDEK also wrote the libretto for BETWEEN TWO WORLDS, with music by Pulitzer Prize-winning composer SHULAMIT RAN, commissioned and produced by the Lyric Opera of Chicago. KONDEK and RAN have just finished up a new version of THE DIARY OF ANNE FRANK, which will premiere at Indiana University.

In addition to BORGIA INFAMI, his collaborations with composer HAROLD BLUMENFELD include the farcical ensemble opera FOURSCORE, the opera-bagatelle BREAKFAST WALTZES, and SEASONS IN HELL, whose libretto was described by the American Record Guide as “an ingenious display of literary virtuosity.” The Cincinnati Enquirer called the SEASONS IN HELL “a fascinating coming-together of words, music and imagery.”
